About

I am a plaintiff-side personal injury attorney. I represent individuals who have been seriously harmed and are navigating one of the most difficult periods of their lives. My approach to advocacy is deliberate, trial-oriented, and grounded in careful case evaluation. I believe that effective representation begins with listening, credibility, and preparation—not volume.

My practice is built on the principle that good lawyering requires both technical competence and genuine care for clients. I invest time in understanding not just the legal issues, but the human impact of what clients are experiencing. This dual focus—on both the law and the people—guides how I approach each case.

Experience

Before becoming an attorney, I worked in personal injury law supporting catastrophic injury cases. During that time, I gained hands-on exposure to client intake, medical records analysis, mediation preparation, and trial support. That early experience shaped how I approach injury cases today: with an emphasis on clarity, organization, and explaining complex facts in terms juries and clients can understand.

My practice focuses exclusively on personal injury matters, representing individuals who have been harmed through the negligence or wrongdoing of others. I work on investigation, discovery, and legal research to build cases that are fact-driven, document-anchored, and resilient under scrutiny.

Philosophy

I practice law because I believe in the importance of the civil justice system and the role it plays in holding wrongdoers accountable. I take cases where I can make a meaningful difference for clients who have been harmed. I do not take every case that comes through the door, and I am transparent with potential clients about the strengths and weaknesses of their matters. This approach allows me to focus my time and resources on cases where I can provide the most value.
